Journey

A year-by-year path from college builder to full stack engineer.

2017

College foundations

Began my computer science journey by learning C, CPP, data structures, and programming fundamentals. I built small academic utilities and discovered how much I enjoyed solving practical problems with code.

2018

Competitive coding and problem solving

Started taking competitive programming seriously through platforms like CodeChef and contests such as ICPC. This phase sharpened my problem-solving mindset, strengthened my grasp of algorithms and data structures, and gave me the confidence to approach engineering challenges with more speed, clarity, and discipline.

2019

Internships and real-world development

Completed my first internship, where I worked with React and Node.js to build reusable UI components and API endpoints. This was the year I started thinking in terms of scalability, version control, and collaboration.

2021

Graduation and career launch

Graduated from college and stepped into my first full-time role as a software engineer. I contributed to customer-facing dashboards, built internal tools, and became comfortable with React, Express, SQL, Git workflows, and sprint delivery.

2022

Owning full stack features end-to-end

Took ownership of features across the stack, from frontend architecture to backend services and deployment. I implemented authentication flows, reporting modules, and performance improvements that reduced API latency and improved user adoption.

Implemented Google Maps integration to improve the location selection experience, replacing manual location input with a more intuitive map-based workflow. This made address selection easier for users and improved accuracy in capturing location data.

2023

Scaling products and cloud systems

Expanded into cloud infrastructure and DevOps workflows, using AWS, Docker, and CI/CD pipelines to make releases smoother and safer. I also partnered more closely with designers and product managers to shape roadmap features from discovery to launch.

2024

Career shift into sports and fitness

Made a major career move by switching domains from healthcare to sports and fitness. This transition gave me the opportunity to apply my full stack experience in a new product space, adapt quickly to different user needs, and contribute to platforms focused on performance, engagement, and digital experiences in the fitness ecosystem.

2025

Five years in and driving impact

With five years of professional experience, I focused on building high-impact products that balance business needs with developer experience. My role evolved beyond shipping code into system thinking, technical ownership, and helping teams move faster with confidence.

Created a new trainer support portal for listing within the organization to improve visibility, discoverability, and interaction between trainers and users. The platform made it easier for customers to explore trainer profiles, connect with the right experts, and engage more directly with the fitness ecosystem.

2026

Building a collaboration platform in progress

Started building an organization-focused chat application inspired by Discord, designed to improve communication and collaboration across different user contexts.

The product is being structured around three workspace types: Self for personal use, Community for open interaction, and Organization for structured team collaboration. It is currently a work in progress focused on creating a more connected digital experience.
